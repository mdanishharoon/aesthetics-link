## Design Context

### Users
Primarily end customers — skincare-curious consumers browsing on their own time, often on mobile, in a relaxed or considered state of mind. A secondary audience of clinic/B2B buyers exists but the primary experience is built for the individual shopper. Context: leisure browsing, considered purchasing, not rushed. They expect the interface to feel as curated as the products themselves.

### Brand Personality
Composed, warm, editorial. Not "premium and elegant" (too dead) — more like a well-edited magazine that also sells things. The brand should feel like something you'd find at a considered concept store: understated, self-assured, unhurried. The interface should never try too hard.

### Reference
TrueKind Skincare (truekindskincare.com) — key signals from their design:
- Bold heavy sans-serif uppercase for declarative statements paired with flowing italic serif for product/concept words. Drama through contrast, not through effects.
- White-dominant base with warm sage green and peach/beige as atmospheric accents — never cold
- Generous whitespace. Things breathe. Nothing is crammed.
- Asymmetric, left-aligned editorial layouts. Not everything centered.
- Thin organic arcs and curved lines as decoration — drawn, not digital
- Large, confident portraiture as the primary visual
- Floating white feature cards with minimal shadows and center-aligned descriptive copy

### Aesthetic Direction
Warm editorial minimalism. Light mode. The palette is skin-adjacent: off-white surfaces, warm charcoal text, soft pink and beige product accents — already established in the codebase. Typography contrast is the primary design tool: Editorial New italic for expressive moments, PP Mori for clear readable structure. Motion should be slow, deliberate, and gravity-aware (things settle, they don't bounce).

### Anti-Reference
Avoid: clinical/cold white + black with no warmth. Avoid: dark mode with glowing accents. Avoid: startup-celebration UI (confetti, green checkmarks in circles, "You did it!" copy). Avoid: identical card grids. Avoid: any pattern that reads as "built by an AI in 20 minutes."

### Design Principles
1. **Warmth before precision** — the palette and type should always feel organic and approachable, never cold or clinical.
2. **Typography IS the design** — use scale, weight, and italic/upright contrast as the primary design tool. Reach for font drama before layout complexity.
3. **Let things breathe** — generous whitespace is not empty space, it is the design. Resist the urge to fill.
4. **Editorial asymmetry** — prefer left-aligned compositions with intentional asymmetry. Centered layouts should be deliberate exceptions, not the default.
5. **Quiet confidence** — no gradient text, no glowing effects, no celebration UI. The interface should feel like it doesn't need to prove anything.
6. **Composed motion** — animations should feel slow, settled, and deliberate. Things decelerate smoothly and come to rest. Nothing bounces.
